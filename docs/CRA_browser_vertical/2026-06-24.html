From the call today, there was a question about what could be a reference document for "restrictions from a source origin on how its resources may be used across origins." It's my understanding that the main WHATWG Living Standards documents won't do because they're unversioned and change freely (the same goes for other widely-used references, such as MDN's docs). I said I thought there were some snapshots and would look into it.

The good news is that WHATWG does publish semiannual "review drafts" (Fetch examples) that are snapshots of the Living Standards. They're stable and easy to get.

The bad news is that almost all of them carry a warning, "Do not reference this version as authoritative in any way." The ones that don't are the 2021 DOM and HTML drafts that got the W3C Recommendation status (this was intended to happen annually, but seems to have fallen by the wayside). And it appears never to have happened for the Fetch Living Standard, which is what we would want to refer to in this case.

This does not seem like an obvious solution to the citation problem, though if we don't mind ignoring the warning, I suppose it could work?
