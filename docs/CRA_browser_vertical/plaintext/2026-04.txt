Hi all, attached is a brief state of the art review regarding current implementations of the *allow-once* option in major browser vendors' permissions system. 

**TL;DR**: All major vendors already implement it, including Chrome and Chromium-based projects (which inherit the functionality), Firefox, and Safari. Therefore, if we do include it as a requirement (for market access essentially), it wouldn't be too disruptive.

I do believe it's worthy of requirement-status (for sensitive permissions, obviously not clipboard or notifications), especially in light of the rise in agentic web browsers.

Happy to discuss

[02:04:04.0743] <Sam>

[02:14:50.0170] <Giovanni Corti (FBK)>
yes, it very aligned with several CRA requirements as well as the CRA's risk-based logic itself. 

Realistically, I don't believe we will face pushback from any major browser vendor for including this.

Additionally, the CRA's drafting preceeds the rollout of agentic web browsers, and is therefore not necessarily designed for the implications that come with it. Even though the CRA is not an AI-specific regulation in itself, i believe the commission will appreciate forward-looking requirements like this. 

In any case, even without the risks of agentic web browsing, i still believe this should be included.
