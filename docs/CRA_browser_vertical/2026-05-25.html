<littledan>
A note about the deadlines/hard requirements:
This deadline has been set by ETSI staff to the rapporteurs which they contract out to (like Denjell, who will be paid only when this is submitted for public enquiry), but the chairs of the ETSI working groups CYBER and CYBER EUSR have firmly rejected the use of deadlines. In turn, ETSI staff actually denied having set deadlines, and agreed with this statement by the chairs. The standard is done when it is fit for purpose, according to WG leadership, not when ETSI staff or their contractors say a date has been reached. The European Commission has a contract with ETSI which requires that the work item be fully standardized by October 31st, but this does not play a role in Working Group procedures, which are based solely on whether the WG agrees that its content is ready.
The "final draft" may be submitted based on the committee's approval, not at the rapporteur's sole discretion. You all are members of the working group. If there is a disagreement within this group, then it can be escalated to the broader CYBER EUSR group. After this group's approval, the CYBER EUSR and CYBER committees will review it and decide whether it is suitable for a "final draft" to be submitted for public enquiry. I encourage you to attend those meetings and give your point of view, rather than delegate this entirely to the rapporteur, especially in the case of disagreements.
I encourage you to be careful with what you submit for a final draft. My preference, and I've expressed this to ETSI staff and chairs, is that the community have a month to review the "final draft" of the browser specification before it is submitted for "Public Enquiry", at which point changes will not be permitted based on comments from this committee or the community directly, but rather from comments agreed on in a national body, which must agree internally on the contents of each part of the comment--a significant barrier to getting information through. It's up to this group collectively, not the rapporteur or an artificial deadline, to determine whether the document is ready for that phase.
(Minor: ETSI has encouraged this committee to use Markdown, and it would be unfortunate if there is significant "prep for the final document" which is outside of that process, which may change it in ways not reflected in version control and code review.)
