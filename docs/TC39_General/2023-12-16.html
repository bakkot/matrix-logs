Uhm, does the spec (+ AnnexB) disallow --> as the first line in a script? It seems so (--> must be preceded by a line terminator), but all browsers accept eval("-->")

my reading of https://tc39.es/ecma262/multipage/additional-ecmascript-features-for-web-browsers.html#sec-html-like-comments agrees with this; |HTMLCloseComment| is the only nonterminal that can start with --> and is only valid after a |LineTerminatorSequence| (possibly wrapped in /*â€¦*/). And PerformEval step 11 does ParseText(StringToCodePoints(x), |Script|), which per ParseText and The Syntactic Grammar and The Lexical and RegExp Grammars begins with application of the lexical grammar and should fail in the case of text starting with "-->".

All implementations that I tested reject a script starting with "-->", but JSC and SM and V8 incorrectly fail to reject eval input that does so:
